Jack Fehrenbacher

Front-end / Development / Design

My ultimate goal. My mentor throughout my time at Seattle University used to tell his students to, first, find something that they love and, then, learn web development. The idea was that whatever you're truly passionate about will be enriched by a deeper understanding of how the web works and the ability to use the tools to navigate it. I'm fortunate to enjoy many aspects of web design and development in itself, but the tools that I've been given, more than anything, allow me to explore and learn in ways that I could not have considered before. My ultimate goal is to be surrounded by people that can teach me more about this field so that I can continue to grow, explore, and create.

Graphic design was my gateway into the world of web development and technologies. The end game was always to create things that did things, to put it bluntly. Learning how to make beautiful, responsive, intuitive websites is my first step into the world of interactive design.

I have some useful skills! I spent the first two years of my academic career in Seattle University's digital design department, and I've spent the last year of my life in the School of New and Continuing Studies' Web Application and Technologies program learning how to bring my designs to life.
